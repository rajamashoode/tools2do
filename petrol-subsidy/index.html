What is the Sasta Petrol Subsidy Scheme?

The Chief Minister Petrol Relief Scheme (frequently searched as the Maryam Nawaz Bike Scheme or Sasta Petrol Program) is a highly targeted government initiative designed to shield lower and middle-income citizens from the global surge in petroleum prices. Unlike blanket subsidies that apply to everyone, this scheme directly targets the primary mode of transport for the working class: motorcycles and rickshaws.

By leveraging digital infrastructure and partnerships with Pakistan State Oil (PSO) and other registered petrol pumps, the government ensures that the subsidy reaches the end-user seamlessly via a QR code system, effectively eliminating the middleman and preventing fuel hoarding.

Detailed Eligibility Criteria for Petrol Relief

To ensure that the subsidy reaches deserving citizens and prevents exploitation, the government has set strict eligibility criteria. Before you apply via the 9771 SMS portal, ensure you meet the following conditions:

  • Vehicle Type: You must own a registered motorcycle (engine capacity between 70cc and 125cc) or a registered commercial rickshaw. Cars, vans, and heavy bikes (above 125cc) are strictly excluded from this scheme.
  • Ownership Integrity: The vehicle must be registered in your name.
  • SIM Card Verification: The mobile number you use to send the SMS to 9771 must be legally registered to your own CNIC.
  • Income Threshold: The applicant’s monthly household income must be verifiable as under Rs. 60,000.

⚠️ The "Open Letter" Warning

Thousands of citizens ride motorcycles purchased on an "Open Letter" (meaning the official ownership was never transferred in the Excise & Taxation records). You cannot claim the petrol subsidy on an open letter bike. To facilitate citizens, the government is currently offering free or heavily discounted ownership transfers at local Excise offices. Transfer your bike immediately to qualify.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Apply Online & via SMS

The registration process has been digitized to make it as accessible as possible. You have two primary methods to register for the Sasta Petrol Relief:

Method 1: The 9771 SMS Format (Recommended)

This is the fastest method and does not require a smartphone or internet connection.

  1. Open the standard messaging application on your mobile phone.
  2. Create a new message and type your 13-digit CNIC number. Crucial: Do not include any dashes or spaces. (Example: 3520112345678).
  3. Send the message to the official government shortcode: 9771.
  4. Wait for the reply. The automated system will cross-reference your CNIC with Excise and NADRA databases. If eligible, you will receive a confirmation message containing a secure PIN or a link to your digital QR code.

How to Claim the Subsidy at the Petrol Pump

Once you are registered and declared eligible, getting the discounted petrol is a simple digital transaction at the station:

  1. Visit any PSO or designated participating petrol pump.
  2. Tell the attendant you want to use the CM Petrol Subsidy.
  3. Show the attendant the QR Code (or provide the OTP PIN) you received via SMS from 9771.
  4. The attendant will scan the code using a specialized Point of Sale (POS) machine.
  5. The system will deduct the amount from your monthly 20-litre quota, and you will instantly pay Rs. 100 less per litre for the fuel dispensed.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is this scheme replacing the Federal Ehsaas Petrol Program?

The Federal Ehsaas Sasta Petrol and Sasta Diesel scheme was a nationwide initiative by a previous administration. The current 2026 CM Petrol Relief Scheme is a provincially managed initiative (spearheaded in Punjab). Even if you were registered in the old Ehsaas program, you must re-register using the new 9771 system.

What happens if I don't use my 20-litre quota in one month?

The quota is strictly calculated on a monthly basis. Unused litres do not roll over to the next month. On the 1st of every month, your quota will reset back to 20 litres.

My SMS to 9771 is failing. What should I do?

If your SMS to 9771 is not sending, ensure you have sufficient standard SMS balance (it is not a toll-free number and usually costs standard network rates). Secondly, ensure your SIM is registered on the exact CNIC you are sending. If problems persist, try applying via the online web portal linked above.
