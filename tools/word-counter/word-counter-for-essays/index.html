Use the Essay Word Counter

Why use a word counter for essays?

Academic essays, university assignments, scholarships and college applications often have strict word limits. Going over or under the limit can affect your grade. This word counter for essays shows your exact word count, character count, sentence count, paragraph count and estimated reading time so you can check your essay before submitting.

How to count words in your essay

  1. Copy your full essay text from your word processor.
  2. Paste it into the box above.
  3. Click Count Now to see all stats instantly.
  4. Edit your essay and recount until it fits your word limit.

Common essay word limits

GCSE and A-Level: 800–2,000 words | University essay: 1,500–5,000 words | Scholarship essay: 250–650 words | College application: 250–650 words | Personal statement: 500–4,000 characters

Common use cases

  • Checking a university assignment meets the required word count
  • Verifying a personal statement fits within the character limit
  • Counting words in a scholarship essay before final submission
  • Tracking word count while writing to avoid going over the limit

Frequently asked questions

Does the word counter count hyphenated words as one or two?

Hyphenated words like "well-known" are counted as one word because they form a single unit. Most word processors follow the same rule.

Are headings and footnotes included in the count?

Whatever text you paste into the box is counted. If you include headings and footnotes in your paste, they will be counted. Paste only the body text if your institution excludes them.

Does this word counter work offline?

The tool runs in your browser, but you need an internet connection to load the page. Once loaded, the counting itself runs locally in your browser.

Is my essay text uploaded to a server?

No. All counting is done directly in your browser. Your text stays on your device.
