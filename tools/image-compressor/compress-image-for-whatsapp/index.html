Why compress images before sending on WhatsApp?

WhatsApp automatically compresses photos when you send them, which can reduce quality. By compressing images yourself before sending, you control the output quality and file size. Smaller images also load faster for the recipient, use less mobile data and are quicker to send in groups or broadcasts.

How to compress an image for WhatsApp

  1. Choose your photo from your device.
  2. Adjust the quality slider — lower values give smaller files.
  3. Click Compress for WhatsApp.
  4. Check the before/after file size shown and download if satisfied.

Recommended file sizes for WhatsApp

Profile picture: under 300KB | Shared photo (good quality): 200–500KB | Document image: under 1MB | WhatsApp maximum image size: 16MB

Frequently asked questions

Does WhatsApp compress images I send?

Yes. WhatsApp applies its own compression unless you send as a Document (not a Photo). Compressing beforehand gives you more control over the result.

What quality setting should I use?

72–80% quality is a good balance between file size and visible quality for most photos. Go lower for very large photos that need significant size reduction.

Is my photo uploaded to a server?

No. All processing happens in your browser. Your photo never leaves your device.
