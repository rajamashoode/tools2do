Remove Line Breaks from Text

Why remove line breaks from text?

When you copy text from PDFs, emails, word processors or websites, it often brings along hard line breaks that do not belong in your destination. This causes text to appear broken when pasted into HTML, social media, email templates or databases. This tool removes all line breaks or just paragraph breaks, giving you clean single-block text.

How to remove line breaks online

  1. Copy your text and paste it into the input box.
  2. Click Remove Line Breaks to remove all breaks, or Remove Paragraph Breaks Only to keep single line breaks but remove double-spaced gaps.
  3. Copy the clean output for use in your document, email or platform.

When to remove line breaks

Pasting PDF text into a web editor | Cleaning copied book or article text | Preparing content for an email or CRM template | Normalising text from spreadsheet cells | Cleaning OCR or scanned text output

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a line break and a paragraph break?

A line break (Shift+Enter in most editors) moves to the next line within the same paragraph. A paragraph break (Enter) creates a new paragraph with spacing above and below. Both appear as newline characters in plain text.

Will removing line breaks affect my formatting?

Yes — the result is a single block of text. If you need to preserve paragraph structure, use the "Remove Paragraph Breaks Only" option to only remove double line breaks.

Is this the same as Remove Extra Spaces?

Related but different. Remove Extra Spaces handles multiple spaces and tabs. This tool specifically targets newline characters (Enter/Return presses).
