Facebook image size guide

Facebook crops and compresses images that do not match its recommended dimensions. Using the correct size prevents blurry crops, white borders and quality loss. This tool lets you resize to any Facebook preset or enter a custom size — all processing happens in your browser.

How to resize an image for Facebook

  1. Choose a preset from the dropdown (Post, Cover, Profile, Story) or enter custom dimensions.
  2. Select your image file.
  3. Choose the output format (JPG recommended for photos).
  4. Click Resize Image and download the result.

Facebook image size reference

Facebook Post: 1200×630 px | Cover Photo: 820×312 px | Profile Picture: 180×180 px | Square Post: 1200×1200 px | Story: 1080×1920 px

Frequently asked questions

Why does Facebook still compress my image after resizing?

Facebook applies its own compression to all uploads. Using the recommended dimensions reduces the amount of resampling, which minimises quality loss. Save as JPG at high quality for best results.

What is the best format for Facebook images?

JPG is recommended for photos and cover images. PNG is better for graphics with text or transparent backgrounds. Avoid uploading very small or very large images.

Can I use this for Facebook ads?

Yes. Facebook ad images typically use 1200×628 or 1080×1080 px. Use the custom size fields if the preset does not match your exact ad spec.
