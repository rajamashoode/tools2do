What is Title Case?

Title Case is a capitalisation style where the first letter of most words is capitalised. Small connecting words like "a", "an", "the", "and", "but", "or", "for", "in" and "of" are kept in lowercase unless they appear at the start of the title. This converter follows standard title case rules used in journalism, publishing and SEO content writing.

Frequently asked questions

What words are kept lowercase in Title Case?

Short prepositions and conjunctions like "a", "an", "the", "and", "but", "or", "for", "in", "of", "at", "by", "to" are kept lowercase unless they begin the title.

Is Title Case the same as Sentence case?

No. Sentence case only capitalises the first word and proper nouns. Title Case capitalises the first letter of most words.

Does this follow AP or Chicago style?

This converter follows a standard widely-used rule set that matches most style guides for web and publishing. Specific style guides like AP or Chicago may have minor differences for certain words.
