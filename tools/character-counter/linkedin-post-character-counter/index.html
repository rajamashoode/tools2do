LinkedIn character limits explained

LinkedIn has different character limits for different content types. Knowing the limits helps you write posts that display well, avoid unexpected truncation and keep your messaging clear. This LinkedIn character counter shows your count instantly and tells you which limit bracket your content falls into.

How to count LinkedIn post characters

  1. Write or paste your LinkedIn content into the box.
  2. Click Count Now to see the character and word count.
  3. Read the automatic guidance for your content type.
  4. Edit and recount until you're satisfied.

LinkedIn character limits reference

Post (feed): 3,000 characters | Article: 40,000 characters | Headline: 220 characters | Summary/About: 2,600 characters | Connection request note: 300 characters | Comment: 1,250 characters

Common use cases

  • Checking a LinkedIn post before publishing to avoid getting cut off
  • Verifying a profile headline fits within the 220-character limit
  • Measuring article draft length against the 40,000-character ceiling
  • Counting connection request notes to stay within 300 characters

Frequently asked questions

What is the LinkedIn post character limit?

LinkedIn feed posts can contain up to 3,000 characters. The first ~700 characters are visible before readers click "see more", so the opening of your post matters most.

Does LinkedIn count spaces and line breaks?

Yes. LinkedIn counts all characters including spaces, line breaks and emoji. Emoji may count as 1–2 characters depending on the character.

What is the LinkedIn headline character limit?

Your LinkedIn headline can be up to 220 characters. Most users see only the first 60–90 characters in search results and profile cards, so lead with the most important information.
